About the Freelance Hourly Wage Calculator

What is the Real Hourly Wage Calculator?

The Real Hourly Wage Calculator is a free, privacy-focused financial tool built specifically for independent contractors, consultants, and agency freelancers. It helps you determine your true "take-home" hourly pay for fixed-fee projects by stripping away overhead costs and tax burdens. Because financial data is highly sensitive, this tool operates entirely on the client-side. Your income numbers, tax rates, and project fees are never uploaded to a database or tracked by external servers.

The "Gross vs. Net" Freelancing Trap

When pricing a flat-rate freelance project, it is dangerously easy to fall into the trap of simple division. If you charge $1,500 for a website that takes 20 hours to build, it feels like you are making a comfortable $75 per hour. However, traditional W-2 employees have their taxes handled automatically and their equipment paid for by the company.

As a freelancer, you are running a business. You must account for self-employment taxes, software subscriptions, and project-specific assets. Once you deduct these hidden costs, your actual profit margin is significantly lower. This tool prevents you from underpricing your services by giving you a realistic view of your true profitability.

How to Calculate Your True Rate

To accurately gauge whether a project is worth your time, enter the following parameters:

  • Project Fee: The total gross amount you are billing the client before any deductions.
  • Estimated Hours: The total time you expect to spend on the project. Tip: Be sure to include non-billable time like client kickoff calls, email correspondence, and final revisions.
  • Software/Asset Costs: Your direct project overhead. Did you have to buy a premium font, pay for cloud hosting, or purchase stock video footage to complete this specific job? Enter those expenses here to deduct them from your gross pay.
  • Estimated Taxes: Your combined income and self-employment tax rate. Depending on your country and state/province, this typically ranges from 15% to 30%. Setting this aside ensures you aren't spending money that belongs to the government.

When to Use This Tool

Use this calculator during the proposal phase of a new client contract. By working backward from your desired hourly take-home pay, you can easily figure out exactly what flat-rate fee you need to quote a client to remain profitable.
